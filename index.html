By combining these two symbols, the Ankh represents the hope of eternal life after death. It was believed that the Ankh could open the gates to the afterlife and protect the deceased on their journey to the next world.

The Ankh is a well-known symbol representing life, regeneration, and eternity. Its shape combines the Tau cross, a symbol of life, with the circle, a symbol of eternity. In ancient Egypt, gods were often depicted holding the Ankh, and it was believed to ensure eternal life in the afterlife.

古代エジプトでは、神々がアンクを手に持っている姿が描かれることが多く、死後の世界でも永遠に生きられると考えられていました。
